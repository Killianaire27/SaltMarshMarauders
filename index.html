Days of Operations

Built for the Sea Lords Raider role — heavy shock troops running two-handed weapons and bows, closing distance fast and hitting hard. Three training pillars:

Explosive Rotational Power: Two-handed weapons live and die by hip-to-shoulder rotation. The strike is generated from the ground up — hips, core, then arms.
Loaded Endurance: Gambeson + chainmail + leather at 15–20 lbs means sustained effort under armor weight across 5+ hour operation blocks for three straight days.
Multi-Day Recovery: Day 1 is easy. Day 3 is where battles are decided. This program conditions the body to still hit hard on day three.
Choose Your Track

Training Tracks

Every exercise has a prescription for each track. Find where you honestly are today — not where you want to be — and work that column. The goal is to arrive at the event healthy and ready to fight. Every track achieves that.

Advanced Track
Consistently training with a solid base of strength and conditioning. Program taken as written at full volume and loads.
Athlete Return Track
Strong athletic background, returning after a significant break. Strength ceiling is high and will come back fast — conditioning is ramped deliberately to protect connective tissue.
Intermediate Track
Reasonably active but without a structured program. Work at roughly 80% of Advanced volume and weight. Focus on building the habit and moving well before chasing load.
Foundation Track
Starting from the beginning or close to it. Movement quality over everything. Never chase another track's numbers — this track builds the base everything else runs on.
★ Knee Modification Protocol — For Anyone with Knee History

Regardless of track, if you have lingering knee pain or a prior injury, apply these additions to every Day A and Day C warm-up, and use substitutions marked ★ Knee Mod:

  • Terminal Knee Extensions (TKE) with resistance band: 3×15 each leg — anchor band at knee height, step back for tension, gently press knee to full extension. Do these before any leg work.
  • Quad Set Holds: Seated, rolled towel under knee, squeeze quad to press it down. Hold 5 sec × 10 reps.
  • Ice the knee 10–15 min post-session if it flares. Pain above 4/10 = stop and use the listed substitute.

"Sharpen the blade, don't dull it. Arrive fresh. Arrive sharp."

The Most Common Taper Mistake

The temptation to push harder in the final weeks is real — especially on the Advanced track. Resist it. Fitness gains from a hard session now will not arrive before the event. The damage can. This phase is about arriving healthy.

Taper Week Priorities
  • Sleep: 8+ hours. Worth more than any session.
  • Hydration: Pre-load in the 24 hours before event day. Armor in October South Carolina heat will dehydrate you faster than you think.
  • Food: Eat protein and carbs normally. This is not the time to cut. You need fuel reserves.
  • Mobility: Replace skipped sessions with 15–20 min of light stretching or foam rolling.
  • Don't: Test maxes, introduce new movements, or try to catch up on missed training.
  • Event weekend: Bring real food. High protein, easy carbs. Don't rely on a food truck for 5-hour battle days.

Commander's Notes

Special Protocols

⚓ Athlete Return Track — Managing the Comeback

Strong athletic background means the strength ceiling comes back fast — faster than it should feel safe. The trap is running conditioning at the same pace.

  • Strength: Match the Advanced track from Week 1. This is appropriate.
  • Conditioning: Start at Intermediate pace. Progress to Advanced by Week 10 — not before, even when it feels easy. Connective tissue adapts slower than your cardiovascular system will let you believe.
  • Watch for: Patellar tendon and lower back overuse signals from sudden volume increase. Any pain above 3/10 that persists two or more days = back off.
  • Disrupted sleep: On consistently short-sleep nights (new parent, shift work), scale back Day C intensity specifically. Don't skip — just don't push hard on 4 hours of sleep.
⚓ Foundation Track — The Most Important Rules

This track has the most to gain and the most to protect. Progress is built on consistency and quality — not speed.

  • Rule 1: Never miss two sessions in a row. Missing one is life. Missing two is losing momentum.
  • Rule 2: The correct weight is the one that lets you finish all reps with good form. Not the weight someone else is using.
  • Milestone check-ins: Week 6, Week 14, Week 20. Compare to your own baseline — not anyone else's.
  • The goal: Arrive at the event able to move in armor for 3 days without getting hurt. That is a complete victory.
