Daily Focus

What Each Day Does

Day A — "The Anvil"
Lower Body + Posterior Chain
Squat, deadlift, hip thrust, calf raises, loaded carries. Builds the legs and hips that don't quit in hour 4.
Day B — "The Hammer"
Upper Body + Weapon Mechanics
Press, pull, rotational chop, bow draw sim, grip work. Builds the strike, the draw, and the endurance to repeat them all day.
Day C — "The Raid"
Full Body Battle Circuit
Swings, chops, conditioning, loaded movement. The most event-specific day. Phase 3: wear your kit.

Quick Reference

Key Milestones

Week 6
Phase I ends. Self-assess — how are movement patterns? Foundation track: your first milestone check-in.
Week 7
Phase II begins. Increase weights 5–10%. Bow draw simulation and Pallof press added to Day B.
Weeks 9–12
Armor sim begins on Day C. Advanced/Return: Wk 9. Intermediate: Wk 10. Foundation: Wk 12.
Week 10
Athlete Return track: conditioning progresses to Advanced pace. Not before this week.
Week 14
Phase II ends. Assess strength gains. Foundation track: second milestone check-in.
Week 15
Phase III begins. Pack weight → 15–20 lbs (Adv/Return). Day A conditioning now with pack. Battle Conditioning Finisher added to Day C.
Weeks 19–21
Wear actual gambeson + partial kit on Day C. If kit isn't ready yet, pack stays at max weight.
Week 20
Foundation track: final milestone check-in. Compare to your Week 1 and Week 6 baselines.
Week 22
Taper begins. Cut to 60% volume — same weights, fewer sets. Drop all conditioning finishers.
Week 24
Drop to 2 sessions only (Day A + Day B). Skip Day C entirely. Prioritize sleep and mobility.
Week 25 (Event Week)
One light full-body session Mon or Tue. 30% volume, light weight. Then rest, eat well, sleep 8+ hours, and pre-hydrate. Show up ready.
